# ArcNote 408 Explainer Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

Clean Chinese postgraduate exam classroom explainer. Use a quiet light paper canvas, crisp black text, strong information hierarchy, and a fixed visual grammar: left-side progress rail, center concept board, right-side exam cue. Motion should feel like a well-designed educational animation, not a SaaS landing page or a slide deck screenshot.

## Colors

- `#F6F1E8` warm paper background
- `#171717` ink text
- `#2563EB` blue main signal
- `#0F9F8F` teal progress
- `#F59E0B` amber exam cue
- `#E11D48` rose warning / distinction

## Layout Rules

- Use a stable 1920x1080 board with 96px outer margin.
- Keep the background plain and calm. Do not use checker patterns, oversized watermark words, or decorative gradients behind lesson content.
- Keep title area under 190px height.
- Put captions in a fixed bottom bar and never cover diagrams.
- Use large Chinese text: title 58-72px, labels 30-38px, captions 32-36px.
- Every scene must have one main diagram, not just decorative cards.

## What NOT To Do

- Do not use huge full-width headings that crowd the diagram.
- Do not use dark blurry gradient backgrounds.
- Do not place giant faint English/number watermarks behind content.
- Do not use colored checker/grid backgrounds unless the user explicitly asks for that look.
- Do not put text inside nested cards.
- Do not let arrows or subtitles cross over body text.
